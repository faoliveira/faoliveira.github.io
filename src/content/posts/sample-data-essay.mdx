This is a sample data essay. The layout is visual-first — no reading time, no table of contents. Just the content and the experience.

Data essays combine normal prose with scrollytelling sections and wide breakout visualizations. The text you're reading now sits in a standard `65ch` prose container, same as a regular post.

## The Scrollytelling Section

Below is a Pudding-style scrollytelling section. The graphic panel sticks in place while the text steps scroll past it.

### Step One

This is the first step. As you scroll past this card, the graphic panel on the right stays fixed in place. This is the core Pudding pattern — text drives the narrative, the visual persists.

  </ScrollyStep>
  <ScrollyStep>

### Step Two

The second step scrolls into view. In a real data essay, the graphic might update here — a chart highlighting a different region, a map zooming in, an animation advancing. For now, the sticky behavior is the feature.

  </ScrollyStep>
  <ScrollyStep>

### Step Three

The final step. After this, the scrollytelling section ends and normal prose flow resumes. The graphic unsticks as it scrolls out of view.

  </ScrollyStep>
</ScrollySection>

## Back to Normal Prose

After a scrollytelling section, the essay returns to the standard prose container. This is where you'd continue the narrative, add context, or transition to the next visualization.

The Vinizone pattern uses `<Wide>` and `<Full>` components to break content out of the prose container — the same components available in every other post type.

## Wrapping Up

This sample demonstrates the three modes of a data essay:

1. **Normal prose** — standard `65ch` reading width
2. **Scrollytelling** — sticky graphic with scrolling text steps
3. **Breakout** — `<Wide>` and `<Full>` for data tables, charts, and images

The layout ships zero client-side JavaScript. Scrollytelling is pure CSS `position: sticky`.
